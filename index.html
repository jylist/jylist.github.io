About Me

I was propelled into the medical device field when a family member received deep brain stimulation (DBS) surgery to manage her Parkinson's Disease. It was pure magic; an incredible transformation. Since then, my dream has been to give other people second chances like I feel she was given.

I am currently a senior at Cal Poly - San Luis Obispo studying biomedical engineering with a concentration in mechanical design. I have interned with Abbot's Cardiac Rhythm Management division as a Mechanical Engineering Co-Op. There, I dove into validating designs and created fixtures to aid with manufacturing and quality control. Additionally, I have interned with Stryker's Medical division as an Advanced Quality Engineering intern and am currently interning with Stryker's Neurovascular division as an Advanced Technology & Innovation intern. During my time at Stryker, I have cultivated my engineering creativity while at the forefront of medical device innovation.

I have developed the following skills both in academic and leisure settings:

  • Solidworks
  • Creo
  • MATLAB
  • Python
  • Rhino
  • Autodesk Inventor

In my free time, you can find me training for my first half marathon and cultivating my newfound love for bullet journaling. I'm also a huge coffee enthusiast (probably due to growing up in Seattle) and I enjoy cooking.

My education as well as internships in industry have reinforced my desire to improve healthcare and the quality of life for patients. I am looking to pursue a patient-focused role in product development or field clinical engineering after I graduate in June 2019.
